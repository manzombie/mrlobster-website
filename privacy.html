This Privacy Notice describes how LAB MEDIA LTD (doing business as Mr. Lobster) collects, uses, and shares your personal information when you use our services at mrlobster.co.uk. Questions? Email setup@mrlobster.co.uk

01

What information do we collect?

Information you give us directly

We collect personal information you voluntarily provide when you express interest in our services, contact us, or participate in activities on our site.

  • Names
  • Phone numbers
  • Email addresses
  • Contact preferences

We do not process sensitive personal information.

Information collected automatically

When you visit our site, we automatically collect certain technical information. This does not reveal your specific identity but includes:

  • Log and usage data — IP address, browser type, pages viewed, timestamps
  • Device data — device type, operating system, hardware model
  • Location data — approximate location based on IP address

Google API

Our use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Information from other sources

We may collect limited data from public databases and other outside sources to enhance our ability to provide relevant services.

02

How do we process your information?

We process your information to provide, improve, and administer our services, communicate with you, and to comply with law.

  • To deliver our services — providing the WhatsApp automation setup you've requested
  • To respond to enquiries — answering your questions and resolving issues
  • To send administrative information — service updates and policy changes
  • To fulfil orders — managing your setup, payments, and service delivery
  • To protect vital interests — preventing harm when necessary
03

Legal bases for processing

Under GDPR and UK GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Consent — where you've given permission for a specific purpose. You can withdraw consent at any time by contacting us.
  • Performance of a contract — to fulfil our contractual obligations to you
  • Legal obligations — where required by law
  • Vital interests — where necessary to protect safety

We are generally the 'data controller' for personal information described in this notice. When we process data on behalf of our business customers, we act as a 'data processor' and the business is the data controller.

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to gather information when you interact with our site. These help maintain security, fix bugs, save preferences, and assist with basic site functions.

06

Artificial intelligence products

Mr. Lobster offers products powered by artificial intelligence. Our AI products are designed for:

  • AI automation
  • AI bots and conversational agents
  • Natural language processing

We provide AI products through third-party providers including Anthropic and OpenAI. Your input, output, and personal information may be shared with these providers to enable the AI features. 07

International transfers

Our servers are located in the United States. Your information may be transferred to and processed in the US and other countries. We protect such transfers using the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses, copies of which are available upon request.

08

How long we keep your information

We retain your personal information for no longer than 1 year unless a longer retention period is required by law (e.g. tax or accounting obligations). When retention is no longer necessary, we delete or anonymise the data.

09

Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect your personal information. However, no electronic transmission over the internet can be guaranteed 100% secure. You should only access our services within a secure environment.

10

Minors

We do not knowingly collect data from or market to children under 18. If you become aware that we have collected data from a minor, contact us at setup@mrlobster.co.uk and we will delete it promptly.

11

Your privacy rights

Under EEA, UK, and Swiss data protection law, you have the right to:

  • Request access to and a copy of your personal information
  • Request rectification or erasure
  • Restrict or object to processing
  • Data portability
  • Not be subject to automated decision-making
  • Withdraw consent at any time

To exercise these rights, submit a data subject access request or contact us directly.

If you believe we are unlawfully processing your data, you have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

12

Do Not Track
